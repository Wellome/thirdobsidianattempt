Analysis

So, here, we have a broken character - Vergil, in this case - with his very own theme song which puts on a show of his own power while exploring how he is broken mentally. It may seem like a simple song, but when you analyse both the song and the character, it becomes much deeper in both meaning and symbolism, while also rooting Vergil as this character who has been destroyed and is now rebuilding himself while portraying someone different.

Backstory

In his childhood, Vergil's family was - to his belief - murdered by Demons, which was almost right after his father - Sparda - disappeared. During this, he was stabbed by the sword his father gave him, and made to feel weak, and, born out of desperation and a last ditch effort, his demon heritage activated for the first time and saved him. Through this act, he blamed his humanity and himself, for the death of his mother, and the supposed death of his brother - Dante - seeking more and more power, and causing his inferiority complex that continues to haunt him throughout all of the series, right up until the end of Devil May Cry 5. This tragedy parallels Dante's experience, where his mothers last act was to save him, before the demons killed her, leading Dante to blame his demonic heritage entirely for the death of his mother. While Vergil continued to gain power through his demon half, Dante gained power through his human half and his humanity, leading to one of Dante's more complex themes, Devils Never Cry.

In Devil May Cry 3, Dante and Vergil had found out both of them were alive, where Vergil 'lovingly' stabbed Dante, causing Dante's demon blood to activate, meaning both of the Sons of Sparda now have accessed their Devil Trigger. Throughout the remainder of this game, we see how the Sons of Sparda fight over their differences, and, towards the end, even work together to defeat a common enemy. Despite this, Vergil, heavily overestimating his power, casts himself to Hell to attempt to defeat the Demon who killed his family - Mundus.

Despite his beliefs, Vergil failed to defeat Mundus, and was instead captured and tortured for years on end - his heart ripped out, his mind controlled, his body destroyed. After enough time, Vergil was fully controlled, and made into the puppet Nelo Angelo, forced to do as Mundus asked. It was only through Dante that once more did Vergil return, however, Vergil's return was unknown to him, with Dante having believed Vergil to be dead. Due to this, Dante entered into a state of depression through the events of Devil May Cry 2 and the Devil May Cry anime.

After Devil May Cry 1's events took place, Vergil's body was crumbling, collapsing on itself as it struggled to survive, holding itself together with what little power was left. This left Vergil with one option - to reclaim the Yamato, the sword his father gave him, shattered and left in Fortuna until a man with a demonic arm absorbed the sword and repaired it. One month before Devil May Cry 5 took place, Vergil ripped the mans - Nero, Vergil's son, unknown by everyone except Dante - arm cleanly off, leaving Nero on the ground bleeding. Using the Yamato, Vergil split himself in two, separating his weakness - his humanity - from what he believed to be his strength - his demon blood - releasing V, the frail, slowly crumbling human, and Urizen, a demon of unprecedented power. However, this did not last long - only two months, in fact - where, due to V and Urizen's imminent deaths, they were required to remerge back into Vergil.

Okay, here's the actual analysis

Using what we now know of Vergil, we can start delving into the complexities of his theme. In the first line, "Immortal temptation" refers to Vergil's lust for power, due to what happened to his family all those years ago, with it taking over his mind, being the only thing he cares for, condemning him to suffering. Due to this suffering, he is forced to summon the strength of mayhem - mayhem being something we see referenced multiple times throughout this song, alluding to destruction and misery - merely to survive and to not feel weak. Here, we come to the first chorus.

In this chorus, the mask Vergil wears begins to slip - the calm, concentrated person we see falters back into a being of destruction and chaos. We can see the entire chorus as a display of power, as a display of Vergil's full strength, an act of intimidation, and we can also see its other interpretation, a display of pain and suffering, of misery and mayhem. "I am the storm that is approaching" is Vergil acknowledging everything he has done has led to destruction, using a storm as a symbol for this destruction. The next line is a continuation of the same symbolism, however, also showing that Vergil believes he is alone, that he isolated - "Black clouds in isolation". Vergil may be a villain, but he is one that this chorus alone will make you sympathise for due to the tragic nature of his story. Now, Vergil being the reclaimer of his name is a line that can be interpreted, like almost all the song, in multiple ways - he is ashamed of himself, for being controlled, for being weak, for not saving his family, or, he is reclaiming his families name, the one sullied by the disappearance of his father, and the failure of his brother. I prefer the first interpretation for this song in particular, as it works well with the chorus, it continues the display of both power, and Vergil's pain, alongside his misery. With the line "Born in flames", this a reference to both his rebirth in the climax of Devil May Cry 5, and also where he believes the person he is now began - way back when he was a child, trapped in that graveyard with the Yamato stabbed through his body, the same moment his inferiority complex began, the same moment his family was killed, and the new Vergil was reborn. In the very next line, Vergil once more shows the absolute honour he has for his family, and the knowledge he has of his fathers past life - being a demon who killed thousands if not millions of other demons - believing his father to be the true family crest. With Vergil being awakened, it is most likely in reference to his realisation he cannot gain more power through relying purely on his demonic heritage, and that - like Dante embracing his demon half - he must embrace both halves of his heritage to gain more power, unlocking his Sin Devil Trigger in the process. Here we come to yet another line likening Vergil to a phoenix, being a phoenix's ash; however, this is merely likening him to the ash, not to the phoenix itself, likening him more to the fact that he WILL rise again, as he has done before - his death as Nelo Angelo in Devil May Cry 1, him being mind controlled into Nelo Angelo in Devil May Cry 3, and now, him returning from having being split into two in Devil May Cry 5. With "descending misery", we once again see a likening of Vergil to a natural disaster, the storm having caused misery upon all that have been affected, the mere approach being the destiny that is chasing. Focusing more on that line of "destiny chasing time", what does it mean for Vergil? More than likely it means that Vergil believes him to have some sort of... glorious purpose yet to be fulfilled, some sort of destiny that the Son of Sparda has yet to achieve, merely limited by time.

Alright, apologies for the word vomit. It's a long chorus, and my favourite of all time due to the meanings behind it, and because it sounds cool as hell. Now, on to one of my favourite verses of all time. In the first line, "Inherit the nightmare, surrounded by fate", we see a combination of Vergil's despair, and his acceptance of it. "Inherit the nightmare" more than likely refers to Nightmare from Devil May Cry 1, a beast capable of destroying the infinite dimension of Hell, while being "surrounded by fate" would - going off of this interpretation - Vergil's body and mind being controlled by Mundus, and his acceptance of this. The song then states he "Can't run away", acting as a continuation of the previous line, where Vergil is unable to run away from the darkness - the Nightmare, if you will - surrounding him, and corrupting his fate more and more. Through this corruption, Vergil is forced to "Keep walking the line, between the light", the darkness consuming him entirely, believing all good in himself to be lost to that same corruption, being led astray from the fate he once had until his father disappeared. Now, we come to Vergil's resilience, his want to return to the light - and to the person who he believes can save him, who can bring him back from the corruption, return Vergil to this glorious purpose he seems set on.

This chorus is another display of power, and of Vergil's motivation to keep growing, and to keep returning back when he is lost. In the line "Through vacant halls I won't surrender", we know he is fighting, but for what? For himself? To see if he is worthy of being saved? Well, with the three words "eyes of ember", we return ourselves to the sibling rivalry between Vergil and Dante - Vergil being the cold, calculated one, almost like ice, and Dante being the explosive, somewhat destructive one, akin to a burning flame - the truth being revealed is what Vergil is looking for, something that can save him, or more accurately, someone who can save him. The next line continues this, Vergil and Dante's never-ending rivalry being stated as such, fighting each other forever, fighting through the fire and ice. This is what has saved both of them. Once, Dante was lost, after his belief that he killed his own brother again, and knowing Vergil is alive returned him, allowed him to remember not just the rivalry, but the brothership they share. Vergil, however, was once completely lost to Mundus, not in control of even his own mind, trapped inside himself, and because of his brother, he was able to return from death itself. Yet, once more, we return to William Blake's love for double meanings, this one line can also be interpreted as V and Urizen remerging back into one complete being, remerging back into Vergil, remembering everything, gaining more power, reclaiming himself.
